1. (When the rest of Heaven was blue) Of a demon in my view—
  1. From the lightning in the sky As it pass’d me flying by— From the thunder, and the storm— And the cloud that took the form

  1. From the torrent, or the fountain— From the red cliff of the mountain— From the sun that ’round me roll’d In its autumn tint of gold—

  1. Then—in my childhood—in the dawn Of a most stormy life—was drawn From ev’ry depth of good and ill The mystery which binds me still—

  1. From the same source I have not taken My sorrow—I could not awaken My heart to joy at the same tone— And all I lov’d—I lov’d alone—

    • From childhood’s hour I have not been As others were—I have not seen As others saw—I could not bring My passions from a common spring—

ALONE   BY: Edgar Allen Poe
